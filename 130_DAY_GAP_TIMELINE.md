### Attachment Disruption Research

**"Still-Face" Paradigm**: Developmental psychology research proves that infants exhibit measurable distress when familiar caregivers become unavailable. *(Tronick et al., 1978)*

**Bowlby's Attachment Theory**: Sudden, unexplained loss of a primary attachment figure during infancy causes:
- **Protest Phase** (Days 1-7): Crying, searching for missing parent
- **Despair Phase** (Days 7-30): Withdrawal, decreased responsiveness
- **Detachment Phase** (30+ days): **Emotional detachment from missing parent** ‚Äî child stops seeking that attachment figure

**Harper's Timeline**:
- **Days 1-30** (June 4 - July 4): Protest/Despair
- **Days 30-129** (July 4 - Oct 10): **Detachment Phase** ‚Äî Harper's bond with father systematically severed
